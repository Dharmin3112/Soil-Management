Red Soil

Red soil is a type of soil that typically develops in warm, temperate, and humid climates and comprises approximately 13% of Earth's soils.[1] It contains thin organic and organic-mineral layers of highly leached soil resting on a red layer of alluvium. Red soils contain large amounts of clay and are generally derived from the weathering of ancient crystalline and metamorphic rock. They are named after their rich red color, varying from reddish brown to reddish yellow due to their high iron content.[2] Red soil can be good or poor growing soil depending on how it is managed. It is usually low in nutrients and humus and can be difficult to cultivate due to its low water holding capacity; however, the fertility of these soils can be optimized with liming and other farming techniques.[1]

About Crop Nutrition

About Nutrition In Percentage
Red soil gets its color primarily from iron oxides. This is the most prominent nutrient in terms of concentration. Iron (Fe) 10-30%
Aluminum is also significant, especially in lateritic red soils. Aluminum (Al) 5-15%
Silicon is a major component of the soil matrix, though not as prominent as iron. Silicon (Si) 40-50%
Magnesium is present in moderate amounts. Magnesium (Mg) 1-3%
Potassium is present but in relatively smaller amounts. Potassium (K) 1-2%
Phosphorus is often found in lower concentrations in red soils. Phosphorus (P) 0.1-0.5%
Organic matter is relatively low in red soils compared to other soil types Organic Matter 1-3%

Crop Informtion

Crop type Type of season Duration Crops Discription
Pulses and Legumes Kharif June to September Chickpeas, lentils, pigeon peas, and green gram. These crops thrive in the monsoon season when the soil is moist but well-drained.
Oilseeds Kharif June to September Groundnut (peanut), sunflower, and soybean. Oilseeds generally do well in the monsoon season, benefiting from the rain.
Cereals Kharif June To September Sorghum (jowar), pearl millet (bajra), and maize (corn) These crops are well-suited to the rainy season when red soil retains moisture.
Vegetables Rabi October to March Tomatoes, onions, and okra (ladyfinger) For these crops, the cooler months are more favorable, avoiding the high temperatures of the summer
Fruits Rabi and Kharif Throughout the Year Bananas, citrus fruits, and guavas. Most fruit trees can be planted at various times, but they generally prefer a warm climate with well-drained soil.
Root Crops Rabi October to March Sweet potatoes and carrots. These crops benefit from cooler temperatures and less rainfall, which helps in better root development.
